My Story

I'm currently studying full stack development via The Odin Project. It's a 1000-hour long course that teaches HTML, CSS, JS, Git, Ruby, SQL, Rails, Angular, React, and Vue.

But how did I get here? Why programming? Why web development?

If-Then-Else

As a child I played video games. My favourite series was Final Fantasy and I wanted more. When we got an internet connection about 20 years ago, I learned how to download older Final Fantasy games not released in Finland. But it wasn't enough. I wanted to make my own games.

I discovered RPG Maker, a tool to make your own role-playing games.

One of my strongest memories from RPG Maker is the programming concept if-then-else. For example: if a treasure chest is still unopened, then get treasure, else get nothing. I thought: "Wow, this is how games work!"

Loops

As a teenager I started to make my own music. I made rap beats that apparently sounded like video game music. Both genres heavily utilize loops: rap beats are repetitive and video game music usually loops until you reach the next area or event.

Loops are another important programming concept. With if-then-else and loops, you can apparently program anything. So I was well prepared already at a young age :)

Web Development

I studied Media Engineering in university 2008–2014, with a focus on web development. I found it fun and full of potential. I also studied the basics of compter science and created a small music-composing progam in Java.

But I spent several years on "side quests" before getting back to web development.

Japanese + Concerts

I went to Japan in 2009 and fell in love with the language, the country and game music concerts. I decided to learn Japanese and figure out how I could produce game music concerts myself. After a few years of interviewing people involved in concerts I was ready.

In 2016 I founded my own company and produced concerts in Finland, Sweden, and Norway. My biggest event was Helsinki Game Music Festival in 2018. The festival had ~100 musicians (12 different musical groups) and ~1000 attendees, and was broadcasted in Finnish national TV news.

I also worked as a Japanese teacher and translator. I have achieved the highest level in the standardized Japanese-Language Proficiency Test.

Back to Web Development

During my "side quest years" I had web app ideas constantly on the back burner. I planned to develop them as soon as I had the time to learn to program better. But I never had enough time.

In 2018, I decided to pursue a career in full stack development. It's something I enjoy doing in my free time anyway, and it is the fastest path to leveling up my skills. I have interviewed at several tech companies and am extremely inspired.

A love for personal development and happiness at work seems to be common a mindset for tech companies. I cannot wait to be part of a positive spiral of helping with code, learning from seniors, learning proper development, becoming better, becoming more useful, taking on bigger roles, and hopefully helping others grow.

I am currently studying the Full Stack Ruby on Rails -course on The Odin Project. Most of my code is on GitHub. If you are in the Helsinki area and are interested in hiring me, please contact me. Else, thank you for taking the time to read my story. End.
